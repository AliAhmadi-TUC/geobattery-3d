What am I looking at

A geobattery in a flooded mine

This is the surveyed geometry of a research drift in the Reiche Zeche mine, flooded and operated as a seasonal heat store: warm water is injected in summer, held through autumn, and drawn back in winter. The glowing field is the simulated temperature in the surrounding rock — a full-3-D conjugate heat-transfer model of one annual duty cycle (charge → store → discharge → recover).

Arrows trace the water circulating from inlet to outlet; the stress view shows von Mises stress in rock and concrete. The geometry is real, the duty cycle hypothetical.
