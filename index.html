As-built drift, end of charge (year 1). The surveyed drift filled with water at ~66 °C right after the 61-day charge. Over the following months this heat drains into the surrounding rock — a single drift behaves as a monthly buffer, not a seasonal store.

Drift-array unit cell, end of storage (year 10). One cell of a 10 m-pitch array, its faces adiabatic symmetry planes. After ten years the rock between drifts has saturated, so the stored water stays warm (~58 °C): the array keeps almost everything the single drift lost.
