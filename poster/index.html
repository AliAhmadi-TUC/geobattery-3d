The trade-off you cannot dodge

Delivered = captured × RF(Fo). Wait for the demand and the heat is gone; discharge early and there is no demand. The curve is the product, swept over every seal length.

04 · when it does workRead your mine before you dig

Retention collapses onto three dimensionless groups, so absolute size cancels: a mine reads its own first-cycle retention off one chart, from survey data alone, before any 3-D mesh exists. The only geometry that enters is the specific surface a = Awetted/Vwater, or equivalently the reach Lc = 2/a.

04b · gradeThe ground sets the floor, the opening sets the lift

Quantity and grade are different questions. Retention says how much heat comes back; the in-situ rock temperature T₀ says how warm it is when it does. Site the store in warmer ground and the whole curve lifts — but the lift itself, θ, never depends on T₀.

Twater(end of storage) = T₀ + (Thot − T₀)·θ   θ = θ(Fo, κ, χ) — never θ(T₀)

07 · numerical challengesThree FEniCSx lessons — rotate them, then re-solve them

The heat crossing a temperature-pinned inlet is not the nodal k dT/dn — that quantity is mesh-dependent and simply wrong. The true flux is the reaction on the constrained degrees of freedom: assemble the unconstrained row, then evaluate it on the solved field. In 3-D the same pin also collides with the material-interface condition at the shared corner node, which is why the fix is a weak (penalty) statement rather than a second strong one.

Incompressible Stokes makes the Taylor–Hood system symmetric indefinite: the pressure block is zero, so there is no Cholesky and no cheap CG/AMG. A direct factorisation is robust — and it is what caps the mesh. Every number below was measured on this machine.
